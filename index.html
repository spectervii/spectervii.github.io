About Me

I didn't choose technology. I grew into it, one discipline at a time, and I have no intention of stopping.

My journey began with data analytics. Numbers told stories that words couldn't, and I became obsessed with making those stories useful. I learned to clean messy datasets, build dashboards that I, as a decision maker could actually use, and ask the right questions before even touching a spreadsheet. Data taught me how to think precisely.

That precision led me directly into quality assurance. When I understood data, I started seeing where systems broke down, where assumptions didn't hold, where edge cases slipped through, where documentation lied about what software actually did. QA gave me a reputation for finding problems other people missed. It also taught me how to communicate findings clearly to engineers, product managers, and stakeholders.

Cybersecurity came not long after, not as a pivot, but as a natural extension. If you care about data integrity and software quality, you cannot ignore the threat landscape. I began studying network security, vulnerability assessment, and threat modelling. I learned that the best defence starts with understanding how attackers think. Every new concept reinforced something I already knew from a different angle.

Today I sit at the intersection of these disciplines, and I think that's where the most interesting work happens. I understand data pipelines well enough to find their security weaknesses. I understand software testing well enough to build reliable automated workflows. I understand digital marketing analytics well enough to tie technical decisions back to business outcomes. And I'm learning AI, automation, and cloud platforms because the industry is moving and I intend to move with it.

What motivates me is simple: I want to build things that actually work. Not things that sort of work under ideal conditions. Things that are reliable, measurable, and secure when they count. That requires discipline, continuous learning, and a degree of humility, the willingness to admit what you don't know and then go learn it.
